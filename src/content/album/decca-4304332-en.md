## Album Overview

On the evening of July 7, 1990, beneath a full moon over the ancient Baths of Caracalla in Rome, music history witnessed an unprecedented miracle. On the eve of the FIFA World Cup, the three greatest tenors of the era — Pavarotti, Domingo, and Carreras — shared the stage for the first time, joined by conductor Zubin Mehta and an orchestra of over two hundred musicians. This live recording became the best-selling classical album of all time, with worldwide sales exceeding ten million copies.

Released by Decca, this recording faithfully captures every stirring moment of that starlit evening. After each tenor performed individual arias, the concert culminated in a spectacular medley and encore that brought the audience to its feet. The repertoire spans opera selections by Puccini, Verdi, Lehár, and others, alongside Italian favourites such as 'O sole mio', showcasing the most captivating side of operatic singing.

## Solo Performances

### José Carreras

Carreras opens with the Lamento di Federico from Cilea's L'Arlesienne, a compact aria demanding rapid transitions from lyricism to passion, which Carreras delivers with his characteristically crystalline tone. He then performs Cardillo's Core 'ngrato, a Neapolitan song whose aching melody expresses the anguish of rejected love, rendered with exquisite emotional sensitivity. His segment concludes with Lara's Granada, a fiery showcase of激情 and brilliant high notes that sets the stage for what follows.

### Plácido Domingo

Domingo chooses 'O paradis' from Meyerbeer's L'Africaine, a sustained aria that highlights his warm, resonant timbre and masterful breath control. He then offers Lehár's 'Dein ist mein ganzes Herz' from Das Land des Lächeln's, the German lyric melody made deeply moving through his expressive phrasing. Sorozábal's No puede ser adds a dash of Spanish fervour, revealing the heroic, masculine quality of his voice. He closes with Puccini's 'E lucevan le stelle' from Tosca, delivering this anguished aria with heartbreaking intensity.

### Luciano Pavarotti

Pavarotti begins with Puccini's 'Recondita armonia' from Tosca, immediately revealing his signature bright, ringing tone. He follows with Crescenzo's Rondine al nido, a tender little gem that perfectly suits the lyrical side of his voice. Curtis's Torna a Surriento adds Neapolitan warmth to his solo segment. Then comes the concert's most electrifying moment: Pavarotti's performance of 'Nessun dorma' from Puccini's Turandot — the FIFA World Cup anthem rendered with regal authority, his climactic high B piercing the Roman night with stunning power and cementing his legend as the King of High C.

## The Grand Finale

The concert concludes with all three tenors singing together in a brilliant medley arranged by Lalo Schifrin. Beginning with Bernstein's 'Maria' from West Side Story, it weaves through 'Tonight', 'Memory', and other beloved melodies before arriving at the Italian favourite 'O sole mio'. The voices of these three giants intertwine in an unprecedented harmonic tapestry. The evening's final encore — a trio rendition of 'Nessun dorma' — sees all three singers soar to a triumphant high B, bringing this legendary night to a magnificent close.

## Listening Notes

This is one of the most important live recordings in classical music history and a gateway to opera for countless listeners. The sound quality is excellent for its era, with the natural reverberation of the Baths of Caracalla lending majestic spatial depth to the voices. For a compelling listening journey, start with the closing medley to experience the full impact of three legendary voices together, then revisit each singer's solo segment to appreciate their distinct timbres and styles. For further exploration, compare this with the 1991 Paris concert — the second Three Tenors event — to hear how the artists evolved over the course of a year.
